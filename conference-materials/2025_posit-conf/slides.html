Enter: Behavioral Science

What is it? The interdisciplinary study of how people make decisions
(Spoiler: irrationally, yet predictably & consistently)

Classic Example: The Paradox of Choice

Researchers: Sheena Iyengar & Mark Lepper

Setting: Upscale grocery store sampling booth

Design: 24 jams vs. 6 jams display

10x higher conversion with fewer options

  • [75 seconds]
  • With behavioral science!‚Ä¶which is the science of how humans think, feel and act‚Ä¶often in terms of decision making
  • And decades of research shows people are often irrational‚Ä¶but in surprisingly predictable ways
  • For us as developers‚Ä¶this means our strange user behaviors are understandable‚Ä¶and we can design for them
  • Here‚Äôs a classic study that illustrates what I mean
  • [CLICK] Researchers set up a tasting booth at a grocery store‚Ä¶and they varied the number of jams they had on display
  • [CLICK] In the big display condition‚Ä¶there were 24 jams on the table
    • And in the small display condition‚Ä¶there were 6 jams
  • [CLICK] The big display with 24 jams attracted more shoppers than the small display with 6 jams
    • BUT‚Ä¶only 3% of folks who stopped at the larger display bought jam‚Ä¶whereas 30% of people bought jam in the small display condition
  • [CLICK] That‚Äôs a 10x conversion difference!
  • So more choice attracted attention but paralyzed action‚Ä¶
    • And filter-heavy dashboards do the exact same thing

Applied to Dashboards

Core Concepts

  • Cognitive Load
    • Too many choices overwhelm users‚Äô mental capacity
  • Progressive Disclosure
    • Reveal complexity gradually as users need it
  • Framing Effects
    • Same data can tell completely different stories

Dashboard Examples

  • Cognitive Load
    • Start with 3 key filters, hide 15 advanced ones
  • Progressive Disclosure
    • Show summary ‚Üí detailed dive ‚Üí comparisons
  • Framing Effects
    • ‚ÄúRevenue up 15%‚Äù vs ‚ÄúRevenue missed by 5%‚Äù
  • [75 seconds]
  • Now‚Ä¶the paradox of choice is just one of many behavioral science concepts we can apply to dashboards
  • And we don‚Äôt have nearly enough time to cover them all
  • But I wanted to pull out a couple to illustrate how these aren‚Äôt just theories‚Ä¶they‚Äôre engineering principles we can leverage
  • Cognitive Load‚Ä¶this means too many choices overwhelm users‚Äô mental capacity
    • Like RAM‚Ä¶your users‚Äô brains have processing limits‚Ä¶every filter costs processing power
  • Progressive Disclosure‚Ä¶this means revealing complexity gradually as users need it
    • Show vital information first‚Ä¶and reveal more later on
    • Like lazy loading for human cognition
  • Framing Effects‚Ä¶this is how the same data can tell completely different stories
    • ‚ÄúRevenue up 15%‚Äù versus ‚ÄúMissed target by 5%‚Äù
    • Identical data‚Ä¶but the Proj Manager might see success while the CFO sees failure
  • [CLICK] In practice‚Ä¶this means starting with 3 filters not 18‚Ä¶showing summaries before details‚Ä¶and using smart defaults
  • This is how we engineer the user experience as carefully as we would the backend
