Enter: Behavioral Science

What is it? The interdisciplinary study of how people make decisions
(Spoiler: irrationally, yet predictably & consistently)

Classic Example: The Paradox of Choice

  • Researchers: Sheena Iyengar & Mark Lepper
  • Setting: Upscale grocery store sampling booth
  • Design: 24 jams vs. 6 jams display
  • Key Finding: More choice attracted browsers but paralyzed buyers

Study Results

10x higher conversion with fewer options

  • 1 minute
  • Behavioral science studies how people think, feel, and act‚Ä¶often in the context of decision making
  • Surprise‚Ä¶we‚Äôre not rational‚Ä¶but we are still predictable and fairly consistent when we pay attention to the right drivers
  • Let me show you a classic example that mirrors our dashboard problem‚Ä¶Sheena Iyengar & Mark Lepper‚Äôs famous jam study at Columbia
  • They studied people in the field at a fancy grocery store‚Ä¶think Whole Foods or Trader Joe‚Äôs
  • These shoppers walked by a display stand with either 24 jams‚Ä¶(extensive choice condition)‚Ä¶or 6 jams (limited choice condition)
  • More choice drew people in‚Ä¶60% stopped at the big display vs. 40% at the small one
  • But fewer choices drove action‚Ä¶nearly 30% bought jam after the 6-jam booth, compared to only 3% after the 24-jam booth
  • More choice attracted browsers‚Ä¶but paralyzed buyers
  • So what do we learn from this?
    • Too many options look appealing but kill follow-through‚Ä¶exactly what happens with dashboards overloaded with filters

Applied to Dashboards

Core Concepts

  • Cognitive Load
    • Too many choices overwhelm users‚Äô mental capacity
  • Progressive Disclosure
    • Reveal complexity gradually as users need it
  • Framing Effects
    • Same data can tell completely different stories
  • Choice Architecture
    • Guide decisions without restricting options

Dashboard Examples

  • Cognitive Load
    • Start with 3 key filters, hide 15 advanced ones
  • Progressive Disclosure
    • Show summary ‚Üí detailed dive ‚Üí comparisons
  • Framing Effects
    • ‚ÄúRevenue up 15%‚Äù vs ‚ÄúRevenue missed by 5%‚Äù
  • Choice Architecture
    • Smart defaults, logical grouping, visual hierarchy
  • 30 seconds
  • Now the paradox of choice is just one of many behavioral science concepts that directly apply to dashboards
  • [CLICK] Let me show you specific examples of how these apply
  • Cognitive Load: Instead of 18 filters overwhelming users, start with 3 essential ones and hide advanced options
  • Progressive Disclosure: Show high-level KPIs first, then let users drill into details, then comparisons
  • Framing Effects: The same revenue number can be ‚Äúup 15%‚Äù or ‚Äúmissed target by 5%‚Äù - completely different user reactions
  • Choice Architecture: Smart defaults, logical grouping, visual hierarchy guides users naturally
  • These aren‚Äôt just theories‚Ä¶they‚Äôre actionable engineering principles
